DATA DICTIONARY

Circuit - Criminal



Address

Description: Address of defendant.
Variable Type: String
Values: Values here are typically found in this format: City, State Zip (e.g. Richmond, VA 23219). “State” is the postal code abbreviation for a state.


AmendedCharge

Description: The amended charge (if any).
Variable Type: String
Values: See “Charge” for examples.


AmendedChargeType

Description: Amended charge type (if any).
Variable Type: String
Values: See “ChargeType” for examples.


AmendedCodeSection

Description: The amended code section (if any).
Variable Type: String
Values: See “CodeSection” for examples.


AppealedDate

Description: Date of appeal.
Variable Type: Date (yyyy-mm-dd)


ArrestDate

Description: Date of arrest.
Variable Type: Date (yyyy-mm-dd)


Charge

Description: Indicates the given charge.
Variable Type: String
Values: Some examples of the many values this field can contain include:

  • DREDGING W/O PERMIT
  • DRIVE WHILE REV/SUSP
  • POSS OXYCODONE W/INT
  • FORGERY

CodeSection

Description: Virginia Code section applicable to the crime.
Variable Type: String
Values: Values here are often, but not always, structured in the following format: TITLE.SECTION. Some examples:

  • 18.2-111
  • 46.2-1080
  • B.46.2-1157
Refer to the Code of Virginia (see: https://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/) for the text of a code section.


CommencedBy

Description: How the case was brought to the circuit court.
Variable Type: String
Values:
  • Ancillary Misdemeanor - when a district court certifies a felony offense to be tried, the court shall also certify any other connected misdemeanors to be tried at same hearing (so long as both parties consent).
  • Direct Indictment - case does right to trial before an inquiry is completed (circumvents the preliminary hearing).
  • Fail To Appear - when a defendant fails to appear at a scheduled hearing.
  • General District Court Appeal
  • Indictment - official statement of charge
  • Information
  • J&Dr Appeal - appealing a final order from juvenile and domestic relations court
  • Not Guilty By Reason Of Insanity
  • Other
  • Presentment - alternative to an indictment, a presentment is a written accusation prepared and returned by the grand jury from its own knowledge or observation (without any bill of indictment before it)
  • Reinstatement - action to reinstate driver’s license or other license.
  • Reinstatement Of Felony Reduced To Misdemeanor - when a felony charge that was reduced to a misdemeanor is reinstated
  • Waiver - D waives his rights to object to the proceeding.
  • NaN : No entry.


ConcludedBy

Description: How the case was concluded.
Variable Type: String
Values:
  • Dismissal - judge elects to not let case go on any further. A case may be dismissed with or without prejudice.
  • Guilty Plea - when a defendant answers the charges against him or her by admitting to the charges.
  • Nolle Prosequi - when a prosecutor voluntarily ends a case before the trial begins or a verdict rendered.
  • Other
  • Revoked - court revokes the original penalty instated.
  • Transferred - judge moves case to another court, often in a different district.
  • Trial - Judge With Witness - judge decides all questions of fact and law, and witnesses are allowed.
  • Trial - Jury - judge decides questions of law, jury decides questions of fact.
  • Withdrawn Prior To Trial - court decides to not pursue the case after evaluating the merits of the case.
  • NaN : No entry.


ConcurrentConsecutive

Description: When a person is charged with two or more offenses consecutively, they may run concurrently or consecutively. Concurrent is when all sentences run at the same time. If a defendant is charged with a crime in Virginia and charged with a crime in another jurisdiction, he may serve these sentences at the same time. Consecutive is when a defendant must serve the entirety of one sentence before they start serving the other sentence.
Variable Type: String
Values:
  • Concurrent
  • Consecutive
  • NaN : No entry.

Variable Type: String

DispositionCode

Description: The outcome / disposition of the case.
Variable Type: String
Values:
  • Appeal Withdrawn - a party appealing the trial court’s ruling must give written notice of that withdrawal to the court and opposing counsel before the appeal hearing.
  • Dismissed - judge elects to not let case go on any further, so the entire case is dismissed.
  • Guilty - defendant is found to have committed the charges against him or her.
  • Mistrial - trial is found invalid because of an error in the proceeding.
  • No Indictment Presented - In a criminal felony case, a grand jury is assembled to decide if there is enough evidence to proceed to a full trial. If a grand jury finds that no probable cause exists under which to issue an indictment, then the case will be dismissed as “No Indictment Presented.”
  • Nolle Prosequi - the prosecutor officially abandons part or all of the action they initiated by filing Nolle Prosequi notice (the charges are “nol prossed”).
  • Not Guilty By Reason Of Insanity - not guilty because they had a mental illness at the time of the commission of the crime.
  • Not Guilty/Acquitted - defendant is found to not have committed the acts or omissions charged by the prosecutor.
  • Not True Bill - charges dismissed against a defendant because the grand jury didn’t find enough evidence to charge them with violation of a law.
  • Remanded - the higher court sends the case back to the lower court for further action.
  • Resolved - case was resolved through a plea bargain (lowering or changing the sentence in exchange for a plea).
  • Sentence/Probation Revoked - a court can revoke a sentence or period of probation for any cause it deems sufficient that occurred within the maximum period of original sentence or probation period.
  • NaN : No entry.

HearingPlea

Description: Plea entered by defendant.
Variable Type: String.
Values:
  • Alford - Defendant pleads that he is innocent, but that the prosecution has enough substantial evidence to prove he is guilty beyond a reasonable doubt. The defendant makes a calculated decision to plead this way. This usually results in the defendant being found guilty of a lesser crime.
  • Guilty - the defendant denies the charges.
  • Nolo Contendere - a “no contest” plea. The defendant doesnt admit or deny the charges, but has the same effect as pleading guilty. This is often plead as part of a plea deal.
  • Not Guilty - the defendant admits to the charges.
  • Tried In Absentia - criminal proceeding in which the defendant is not physically present.
  • NaN : No entry.

Description: Plea entered by defendant.
Variable Type: String.
Values:

  • Bench Warrant - Withdrawn

  • Bond Denied - court refuses to grant bail (often because defendant presents as a flight risk) and the defendant returns to prison until the next hearing.
  • Bond Forfeited - when a defendant fails to show up for a court appearance and the company or person who put up the bond is required to pay defendants’s outstanding bond amount (usually bail bondsmen are only required to pay a small % of the amount if the defendant doesn’t flee). The court then takes the bond, and it cannot be refunded.
  • Bond Forfeiture Docketed - court schedules a bond forfeiture hearing at a future date.
  • Bond Forfeiture Paid - When the party responsible for paying bail pays the entire outstanding amount.
