Variable Type: String.
Values: Field takes one of the following values:

  • Initial Filing - first time that the party is filing this case with the court.
  • Jdr Court Appeal
  • Other
  • Reinstatement
  • Transferred
  • NaN : No entry.


ComplexCase

Description:
Variable Type:
Values: Field takes one of the following values:

  • Bankruptcy - a debtor files a bankruptcy petition with the court when they cannot repay their outstanding debts.
  • Complex - cases that require more intensive judicial management (i.e. many attorneys, many parties, complicated subject matter, many expert witnesses, extensive discovery, parties from many jurisdictions).
  • Habitual Offender - a defendant who has been convicted of multiple offenses may be declared a habitual offender. Once you are declared a habitual offender, any new infractions will be punished more severely.
  • Information
  • NaN : No entry.


ConcludedBy

Description: Describes how the case was concluded.
Variable Type: String.
Values: Field takes one of the following values:

  • Affidavit For Divorce - file given to court as part of initial request to dissolve the marriage. It is an oath by both spouses that they believe the marriage can’t be fixed and they agree to call off their marriage legally. They may also be required to file a financial affidavit to assist the court in a division of assets later.
  • Default Judgment - one party has failed to perform a court-ordered action and that failure has impacted the case so substantially the judge finds in favor of the other party (ex. When a defendant fails to go before the court repeatedly).
  • Deposition - a sworn out of court testimony conducted by one of the party’s lawyers. Depositions are conducted as part of the discovery process before a trial or settlement. Witnesses can be subpoenaed to attend a deposition, but both party’s lawyers are usually present.
  • Dismissal - termination of the action without a final judgement. In a civil case, this is often achieved when the defendant motions to dismiss the case for failure to state a claim upon which relief may be granted (no real legal or factual basis for the claim).
  • Mediation - instead of revolving issues in a formal court setting with a judge, the parties discuss their disputes with the assistance of a impartial third person(s) who have been trained to be mediators. A mediation is often recommended if the judge believes this might be a better avenue to figure out the issue at hand or the parties schedule it during a settlement conference.
  • Nonsuit - judge decides an action is a nonsuit when a P fails to provide sufficient evidence or make an adequate legal showing for the judge to rule in the plaintiff's favor. It is like a dismissal, but a nonsuit allows the plaintiff to correct a flaw in their case and re-file the action without any penalty.
  • Other
  • Purged After 1 Year - when certain evidence (police reports, admin records, traffic records) or an entire charge in a defendant’s record is expunged (erased or thrown out) after 1 year.
  • Purged After 2 Years - when certain evidence or a charge in a defendant's record is expunged (erased or thrown out) after 2 years.
  • Purged After 3 Years - when certain evidence or a charge in a defendant’s record is expunged (erased or thrown out) after 3 years.
  • Report By Commissioner
  • Resolved - all issues before the court are resolved and there is no need for further judicial intervention.
  • Settlement - both parties agree to settle their issues before a trial in order to avoid lengthy litigation.
  • Settlement/Nonsuit/Vol. Dismissal - for some reason, there is no justiciable issue in front of the court.
  • Transferred - the case is transferred and moved to another court.
  • Trial - Judge With Witnesses - a so-called “bench trial” occurs when there is only a judge and no jury. The ultimate decision maker is the judge, and he listens to witnesses to decide if they are credible and how much to weigh their testimony. These are much more common in civil cases.
  • Trial - Jury - a trial with a jury occurs when it is a jury of the defendant’s peers who decide the ultimate outcome. A jury trial is guaranteed for criminal defendants who are charged with serious offenses that subject the defendant to prison time.
  • Withdrawn Prior To Trial - a party withdraws their action before trial begins.
  • NaN : No entry.
