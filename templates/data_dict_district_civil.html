Garnishee

Description: A third party (usually a bank or employer) who receives a court order to not release funds that are owed to a customer or employee until the court makes a further order. For example, party A owes a creditor a lot of money, but A only has his salary (no savings he can pay the debt with), so the creditor initiates a “Garnishment proceeding” to ensure payment. If this proceeding is successful, a portion of A’s salary is sent to the creditor every month by the employer (who is the garnishee).
Variable Type: String.
Values: Name of bank or employer.


GarnisheeAnswer

Description: The form the garnishee (third party holding a debtor’s property or assets) is required to fill out under oath and in writing. They must return the garnishee answer to the court, debtor, and creditor in a garnishee proceeding. A garnishee proceeding is initiated by a creditor in order to seize the property or assets of the debtor. A court may order the garnishee to hold the debtor’s wages to pay the creditor.
Variable Type: String.
Values:
  • Check Received
  • Written Answer
  • NaN : No entry.


HomesteadExemptionWaived

Description: The Homestead Exemption allows homeowners to protect some of their equity during bankruptcy proceedings ($5,000 from home or personal property covered by the Homestead Act). This Exemption may be waived if a person declares in a bond, bill, note or other instrument that he or she waives the exemption from liability on the property or estate. If the waiver is clear, the property usually exempted under the Homestead exemption is now liable to be subjected for any obligations under that contract.
Variable Type: String / boolean.
Values:
  • f : False
  • t : True
  • NaN : No entry.
