DATA DICTIONARY

District - Criminal



Address

Description:
Variable Type: String.
Value: City, state (postal abbreviation), and zip code.


ArrestDate

Description: Date of arrest.
Variable Type: Date (yyyy-mm-dd)


CaseType

Description: Type of case.
Variable Type: String.
Value:
  • Capias : Failure to appear before the court.
  • Civil Violation : violation of state or local law, other than a criminal offense, that is punishable by a civil fine or forfeiture.
  • Felony : For crimes more serious than misdemeanors. Crime usually involves violence and punishment usually involves jail time.
  • Infraction : Minor violation (i.e. traffic ticket). Usually punished with a fine and does not have a right to a jury trial.
  • Misdemeanor : a criminal offense that is less serious than a felony, and usually punishable by a fine and some time in a local county jail.
  • Motion [18.2-271.1(E)] : Restrict or revoke (?) driver’s license after a DUI charge. Refers to relevant Virginia code section.
  • Non Case - determined to not be a judicial case
  • Other
  • Restricted Operator's License For Fine and Costs Only - a driver will have their license restricted when they violate their driver improvement probation. They can then apply for a restricted operator’s license that allows them to drive for work or school purposes.
  • Show Cause - this is an order that requires one or more of the parties to a case to justify, explain, or prove something to the court (judge needs more information before deciding an issue).

Value: Some examples of the many values this field can contain include:

FinalDisposition

Description:
Variable Type: String.
Value: Any combination of name you can think of, including:
  • Adjudicated Habitual Offender - if you are charged with three major convictions or twelve minor convictions you will be deemed a habitual offender and will not be allowed to drive a motor vehicle within the state.
  • Bond Forfeited - when a court appearance is missed, the company or person that put up the bond will have to pay the full amount.
  • Certified Misdemeanor - if a district court certifies felony offenses to be tried in a circuit court, the court shall also certify any ancillary misdemeanor offense for trial in circuit court.
  • Certified To Grand Jury - bound to be sent over to the Circuit Court (the only court that can hold a full criminal trial), where a grand jury will hear evidence to decide if they will issue an indictment (that will then send the case to full trial).
  • Complied With Law - when a defendant is determined to have complied with the law (by, for example, presenting a proper license or permit or registration card duly issued to him to the court after failing to carry a valid registration ID).
  • Defendant Not Found - Sheriff or process server was unable to locate and serve process papers of the defendant. The papers are returned to the court with a written statement that the defendant was “not found’ and the court cannot try to case unless process is later served.
  • Denied - a motion is denied.
  • Dismissed - lawsuit is closed (no finding of guilt or conviction)
  • Dismissed/Other
  • Extradition Ordered - when one state/jurisdiction requests the removal of a defendant from another state so that he can be criminally tried in that state. Often, this occurs when an individual commits a crime in one state and then flees. If the Attorney General finds that the extradition request has met the legal requirements for extradition, a Governor’s warrant is issued. If Virginia is the state requesting a fugitive be brought back, the Governor formally requests extradition.
  • Extradition Waived - when the extradition request does not meet all of the necessary requirements for extradition to be ordered.
  • Fugitive File - when a warrant, summons or paper from the district attorney’s office was issued for delivery to the D to appear for trial, but the defendant could not be located. They place these cases, in which a case is still pending but D cannot be located, in the fugitive file.
  • Granted - motion is accepted.
  • Guilty - finding that defendant committed the crime (admission of facts contained in the charge).
  • Guilty In Absentia - defendant was not physically in the courtroom during the trial, but is still found guilty by the judge or jury.
  • NaN
  • Nolle Prosequi - Latin for "Not Prosecuted". This means there was not enough evidence to convict the defendant and the case is dropped.
  • Not Guilty - finding that the defendant is innocent.
  • Not Guilty/Insanity - finding that the defendant is innocent by reasons of mental illness/insanity.
  • Other
  • Prepaid - the final amount due is already made by the D before the hearing.
  • Sentence/Probation Revoked - the D violates terms of his probation, and then judge reinstates the original sentence. The probation revocation hearing happens in court without a jury.
  • Transferred To Another Jurisdiction/Court - when the judge transfers the case to another court (for reasons such as efficiency, fairness, etc.)

HearingContinuanceCode

Description: This variable includes the various codes used to indicate why court hearings are rescheduled or postponed.
Variable Type: String.
Values:
  • Advisement : Hearing held after a person is charged with a crime. Defendant is “advised” about their right to representation.
  • Agreement : All parties agree to continuance.
  • Attorney : Attorney for defendant requested to reschedule hearing.
  • Bench Warrant : Issued by a judge when the defendant violates the rules of the court, usually by failing to appear.
  • Capias : Order for arrest and appearance before the court.
  • Clerk : Clerk of court requested to reschedule hearing.
  • Commonwealth : Commonwealth attorney’s requested to reschedule hearing.
  • Continued By Court : Hearing rescheduled by Court.
  • Defendant : Defendant requested to reschedule hearing.
  • Deferred Disposition : “In general, deferred disposition permits a court to withhold imposition of a sentence and place conditions on the defendant that, when met, allow for the charges to be dismissed. Deferred disposition is usually accompanied by the imposition of conditions similar to probation. Upon the satisfactory completion of all conditions, and if no other criminal offenses are committed during the period of deferment, the original charge may be dismissed.” (see: http://vscc.virginia.gov/documents/deferred%20dispositon.pdf)
  • Failure To Appear : Failed to appear before the court.
  • Generally :
  • Motion : Request from defendant for court to reschedule hearing.
  • Officer's Request : Officer requested court to reschedule hearing.
  • Other :
  • Restitution Review : “When a defendant is ordered to pay restitution and placed on active supervision, the probation agency shall notify the court and the Commonwealth attorney of the amount of any unpaid restitution and the last known address of the defendant, 60 days prior to the defendant’s release from supervised probation. This notice shall be in writing, if practicable a copy of this notice is to be provided to the victim. The court shall conduct a hearing prior to the release of the defendant. The court may (a) release the defendant from supervision, (b) modify the period of or terms of supervision, (c) revoke some or all of the suspended sentence or probation, or (d) proceed in accordance with E of §19.2-358.” (see: http://www.courts.state.va.us/courts/gd/resources/manuals/gdman/chapter03.pdf)
  • Trial : the trial is scheduled for a date that would conflict with the hearing.
  • Weather : Hearing delayed due to inclement weather.
  • NaN : No entry.

HearingPlea

Description:
Variable Type: String.
Values: See: http://www.courts.state.va.us/courts/gd/resources/manuals/gdman/glossary.pdf

  • Guilty : “Responsible for committing a criminal offense or a traffic infraction. The word used by an accused in pleading to the charges when he confesses to committing the crime of which he is charged. It is also used by the judge if he finds that the accused committed a criminal offense or a traffic infraction.”
  • Nolo Contendere : ““I will not contest it”. The name of a plea in a misdemeanor case or traffic infraction proceeding, having the same legal effect as a plea of guilty, so far as regards all proceedings in the case, and on which the defendant may be sentenced.”
  • Not Guilty : “A finding or verdict in a criminal case wherein the judge or jury determines that the Commonwealth has not proven that the defendant is guilty of a charge. Also the plea that may be made by a defendant to assert that he or she is not guilty and to demand that the Commonwealth prove its case.”
  • Tried In Absentia : Proceeding in which the defendant is not present.
  • NaN : No entry


HearingResult

Description: Outcome of a hearing.
Variable Type: String.
Values:
  • Continued : Hearing to be continued at a future date.
  • Finalized : Judgment has been rendered but court clerks can and do manipulate this value in order for downstream systems to function properly depending on the type of crime (see: General District Court Manual, http://www.courts.state.va.us/courts/gd/resources/manuals/gdman/chapter03.pdf)
  • Waived : Defendant waives hearing.
  • NaN : No entry


HearingType

Description: Outcome of a hearing.
Variable Type: String.
Values:
  • Adjudicatory : judge determines whether facts as stated in a petition or warrant are true and subsequently makes a judicial ruling.
  • Administrative Hearing : hearing before a governmental agency or administrative law judge (ALJ) that involves matters under the purview of an agency.
  • Appoint Attorney : Court will appoint a public defender to represent the defendant if he has not already acquired representation on his own. Criminal defendants have a right to an attorney in a criminal trial.
  • Arraignment : first step of a criminal proceeding in which the judge reads the official charging document to the defendant to inform him of all the charges against him. They also ask the defendant if he plans to hire his own attorney or would like a court-appointed attorney.
  • Arraignment By Clerk : The clerk of the court formally reads aloud the charges against the defendant and then the defendant enters a guilty or not guilty plea.
  • Arraignment/Bond/Detention/Appointment Of Atty
  • Hearing :
  • Attorney Review : Usually when the D didn’t qualify for court-appointed attorney or did not request one in arraignment, the court will set an Attorney Review hearing. The defendant must either hire an attorney or explain to the court why they have not by the Attorney Review hearing date. Then the court considers whether the defendant still might be eligible for court-appointed counsel or not.
  • Bond : defendant appears before the judge to request that he be released from detention on bail while his trial is pending. The court considers whether defendant is a flight risk or danger to the community. If the judge holds that the D isn’t a flight risk or a danger to the community, then the D is released on pretrial conditions. If he violates those conditions, the bond may be revoked.
  • Civil Hearing : Non-criminal charges, including disputes between people or businesses involving money or torts, are adjudicated in civil hearings.
  • Contested Hearing : A contested case hearing is usually an informal quasi-judicial administrative hearings run by a state agency when a party wans to challenge an agency's individual determination. The purpose is to give state decision makers the information necessary to make a proper decision when they affect an individuals rights, duties, or privileges. A “contested hearing” can also occur when a party wants to challenge a court’s determination, not an administrative agency (i.e. determination of custody rights in a divorce proceeding).
  • Detention : Hearing during which a judge determines whether the accused will be detained, continue to be detained, or released while his case is pending.
  • Dismissed : The Judge dismisses the charges for one of several reasons, including that the defendant does not show up for and does not show good cause for not showing up, the plaintiff withdraws charges, or a party did not request the necessary hearing within the allotted time.
  • Disposition : defendant decides to take a plea offer or bring the case to court.
  • Disposition/Sentencing/Revocation Hearing : A sentencing hearing is where a judge decides the specific sentence after the defendant is found guilty. A revocation hearing is where a judge decides whether to revoke your probation or your parole.
  • Extradition : One jurisdiction delivers a person accused or convicted of committing a crime in another jurisdiction to that jurisdiction.
  • Extradition Hearing : Judge determines whether they right person the state was seeking was arrested.
  • Hearing Waived : a defendant may waive his right to a pre-trial hearing and proceed to trial.
  • Hearing Waived/Prepayment Dismissed Prior To Trial : if a hearing is waived because defendant requested or judge decides for good reason to waive it, the prepayment required will be dismissed.
  • Motion : a hearing held in front of the judge after one of the lawyers in the case has filed a written request for the judge to do something through a motion.
  • Pre-Trial : a meeting between the parties before the official trial. Prosecutors and defense attorneys can file any number of pre-trial motions that exclude evidence or otherwise shape the proceedings.
  • Preliminary : Before any official trial.
  • Preliminary Hearing : The hearing in which the judge decides if there is enough evidence to force the defendant to stand trial.
  • Prepayment : a D can pre=pay a fine (usually traffic violation fine) so they do not have to physically appear in court. The payment acts as a “written appearance.” This prepayment may act as an admission of guilt in a later civil hearing.
  • Re-Hearing : a re-consideration of a cause granted due to some error in the original proceedings. The re-hearing may encompass some new matters.
  • Re-Open : After an initial decision, a defendant can file a motion to re-open a hearing to introduce new evidence. The movant must show good cause for why they didn’t introduce the evidence in the first hearing.
  • Re-open/Re-hearing : After an initial decision, a defendant can file a motion to re-open a hearing to introduce new evidence. The movant must show good cause for why they didn’t introduce the evidence in the first hearing.
  • Restitution Review : a defendant who commits a crime that results in property damage or other loss may be required to pay restitution (to compensate the victim for loss). A restitution review hearing is required so that the judge can review the defendant’s compliance with the restitution terms until the defendant pays it in full.
  • Review Hearing : when court gets a petition of the Department of Juvenile Justice for a hearing concerning a committed juvenile, the court schedules a review hearing within 30 days to review the juvenile's progress report.
  • Review Progress : a Judge reviews the progress of a defendant to comply with his probation terms.
  • Review Progress Hearing : when a defendant is let out on probation, the judge will schedule a review progress hearing to ensure defendant is making an effort to comply with his probation terms.
  • Revocation :
  • Sentencing : when an offender is given their sentence after they plead guilty.
  • Transfer : hearing during which a Judge considers whether to waive jurisdiction over a juvenile and transfer them to adult criminal court.
  • NaN : No entry.

Status

Description:
Variable Type: String.
Value:
  • Adult - 18 or older.
  • Bail - temporary release of an accused person awaiting trial, often on the condition that a sum of money be lodged to guarantee their appearance in court (will be forfeited if they don’t show up).
  • Custody - legal right to act as guardian of a minor child.
  • Fugitive - an accused person who flees the state. A court can retrieve the fugitive through the process of extradition.
  • Minor - under 18 years old.
  • Other
  • Released On Recognizance - unlike bail, the suspect is released without having to pay money. They merely promise, in writing, to show up for later hearings/proceedings.
  • Released On Summons - when someone is given a ticket it is actually an official document (summons) that provides notice to the person that they are accused of a specific crime and provides the information about the time/date for their hearing. Rather than taking someone into custody, the officer just gives the offender the summons, they sign it, and they are released. Summons are usually given for misdemeanors (traffic and moving violations).
  • NaN : No entry.

  • t : True
  • f : False
