ConcurrentConsecutive
Description: When a person is charged with two or more offenses consecutively, they may run concurrently or consecutively. Concurrent is when all sentences run at the same time. If a defendant is charged with a crime in Virginia and charged with a crime in another jurisdiction, he may serve these sentences at the same time. Consecutive is when a defendant must serve the entirety of one sentence before they start serving the other sentence.
Variable Type: String.
Values: Concurrent

DispositionCode
Description: The outcome / disposition of the case.
Variable Type: String.
Values: Appeal Withdrawn - a party appealing the trial court’s ruling must give written notice of that withdrawal to the court and opposing counsel before the appeal hearing.
Dismissed - judge elects to not let case go on any further, so the entire case is dismissed.
Guilty - defendant is found to have committed the charges against him or her.
Mistrial - trial is found invalid because of an error in the proceeding.
No Indictment Presented - In a criminal felony case, a grand jury is assembled to decide if there is enough evidence to proceed to a full trial. If a grand jury finds that no probable cause exists under which to issue an indictment, then the case will be dismissed as “No Indictment Presented.”
Nolle Prosequi - the prosecutor officially abandons part or all of the action they initiated by filing Nolle Prosequi notice (the charges are “nol prossed”). Not Guilty By Reason Of Insanity - not guilty because they had a mental illness at the time of the commission of the crime.
Not Guilty/Acquitted - defendant is found to not have committed the acts or omissions charged by the prosecutor.
Not True Bill - charges dismissed against a defendant because the grand jury didn’t find enough evidence to charge them with violation of a law. Remanded - the higher court sends the case back to the lower court for further action.
Resolved - case was resolved through a plea bargain (lowering or changing the sentence in exchange for a plea).
Sentence/Probation Revoked - a court can revoke a sentence or period of probation for any cause it deems sufficient that occurred within the maximum period of original sentence or probation period.
NaN : No entry.

Variable Type: String.
Values: Alford - Defendant pleads that he is innocent, but that the prosecution has enough substantial evidence to prove he is guilty beyond a reasonable doubt. The defendant makes a calculated decision to plead this way. This usually results in the defendant being found guilty of a lesser crime.
Guilty - the defendant denies the charges.
Nolo Contendere - a “no contest” plea. The defendant doesnt admit or deny the charges, but has the same effect as pleading guilty. This is often plead as part of a plea deal.
Not Guilty - the defendant admits to the charges.
Tried In Absentia - criminal proceeding in which the defendant is not physically present.

Description: Result of hearing.
Variable Type: String.
Values: Appeal Withdrawn - the appeal from the original holding is withdrawn.
Automatic/Programmatic End To The Hearing -

Bond Forfeited - when a D fails to show up for a court appearance and the company or person who put up the bond is required to pay D’s outstanding bond amount (usually bail bondsmen are only required to pay a small % of the amount if the D doesn’t flee). The court then takes the bond, and it cannot be refunded.
Bond Forfeiture Docketed - court schedules a bond forfeiture hearing at a future date.
Bond Forfeiture Paid - When the party responsible for paying bail pays the entire outstanding amount.

Capias Issued - if a criminal D does not show up for court, the judge issues a capias warrant for their arrest. Any officer who can find you will have the power to take you to jail.
Capias Withdrawn - the warrant to arrest you is now void, but this does not mean the charge is dismissed.
Capias-Defendant Arrested - when the individual who was the subject of the capias order is found and detained.
