ConcludedBy
Description: Describes how the case was concluded.
Variable Type: String
Values: Field takes one of the following values:

● Affidavit For Divorce - file given to court as part of initial request to dissolve the marriage. It is an oath by both spouses that they believe the marriage can’t be fixed and they agree to call off their marriage legally. They may also be required to file a financial affidavit to assist the court in a division of assets later.

● Default Judgment - one party has failed to perform a court-ordered action and that failure has impacted the case so substantially the judge finds in favor of the other party (ex. When a D fails to go before the court repeatedly).

● Deposition - a sworn out of court testimony conducted by one of the party’s lawyers. Depositions are conducted as part of the discovery process before a trial or settlement. Witness’s can be subpoenaed to attend a deposition, but both party’s lawyers are usually present.

● Dismissal - termination of the action without a final judgement. In a civil case, this is often achieved when the D motions to dismiss the case for failure to state a claim upon which relief may be granted (no real legal or factual basis for the claim).

● Mediation - instead of revolving issues in a formal court setting with a judge, the parties discuss their disputes with the assistance of a impartial third person(s) who have been trained to be mediators. A mediation is often recommended if the judge believes this might be a better avenue to figure out the issue at hand or the parties schedule it during a settlement conference.

● Nonsuit - judge decides an action is a nonsuit when a P fails to provide sufficient evidence or make an adequate legal showing for the judge to rule in the plaintiff's favor. It is like a dismissal, but a nonsuit allows the P to correct a flaw in his or her case and re-file the action without any penalty.

● Other

● Purged After 1 Year - when certain evidence (police reports, admin records, traffic records) or an entire charge in a D’s record is expunged (erased or thrown out) after 1 year.

● Purged After 2 Years - when certain evidence or a charge in a D’s record is expunged (erased or thrown out) after 2 years.

● Purged After 3 Years - when certain evidence or a charge in a D’s record is expunged (erased or thrown out) after 3 years.

● Report By Commissioner

● Resolved - all issues before the court are resolved and there is no need for further judicial intervention.

● Settlement - both parties agree to settle their issues before a trial in order to avoid lengthy litigation.

● Settlement/Nonsuit/Vol. Dismissal - for some reason, there is no justiciable issue in front of the court.

● Transferred - the case is transferred and moved to another court.

● Trial - Judge With Witnesses - a so-called “bench trial” occurs when there is only a judge and no jury. The ultimate decision maker is the judge, and he listens to witnesses to decide if they are credible and how much to weigh their testimony. These are much more common in civil cases.

● Trial - Jury - a trial with a jury occurs when it is a jury of the D’s peers who decide the ultimate outcome. A jury trial is guaranteed for criminal Ds who are charged with serious offenses that subject the D to prison time.

● Withdrawn Prior To Trial - a party withdraws their action before trial begins.

● NaN : No entry.

In Virginia, the bill of complaint is required in divorce proceedings/ In the bill of complaint, a party lays out all of the facts that the court may consider in the divorce proceedings, including separation of assets and support.

● Bond Appeal From District Court - when a civil D is given an adverse decision, he can appeal this decision. However, simply appealing doesn’t stop a creditor from trying to come after the D for the judgement. Therefore, he is required to rile a “supercedeas” bond with the court to effectively stay the execution/collection of the civil judgement. In other words, he has to pay to stop creditors from coming after him during the appeal.

● Bond Forfeiture - when you post bond and then you fail to comply with the conditions of the bond, the court can then take the money you paid for bond and keep it.

● Bond Forfeiture Appeal – when the court keeps your bond money but you think they made a mistake or disagree with the decision.
